What NOT to do

  • Do not defragment your drive! Most drives nowadays are SSDs and "defragging" is a thing of the olden days when everyone used HDDs. SSDs will automatically re-trim themselves.
  • Do not run any "cleaning" application! There are a few that are actually good (BleachBit), but most of them are rubbish and lie to you, and delete things that should not be deleted. This includes applications like CCleaner or any cleaning/tune up programs. If it costs money, do NOT use it.

Updating

Before doing anything else, you should update everything on your system. This includes running Windows Update, updating your drivers.

Removing temporary files

    • Note: This will remove your recycle bin contents.
    • Note 2: Run this on every drive you have.

Disable startup applications

    • If a process has a generic name like Update.exe, right click the column header and tick the "Command line" option. That will point at what application and folder it actually is.

Applications

    • Go look in the Apps in System Settings and Uninstall a Program in the Control Panel.
    • Also look at Game launchers like Steam and remove games you haven't touched in years.
